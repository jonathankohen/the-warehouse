Frequently Asked Questions

What types of vintage furniture do you sell?

We offer a wide range of vintage furniture, including chairs, tables, dressers, cabinets, sofas, and more from various styles and time periods such as Mid-Century Modern, Art Deco, Victorian, and more.

What condition can I expect the furniture to be in?

We take pride in offering vintage furniture in the best possible condition. While some pieces may have minor wear consistent with their age, we ensure that all items are structurally sound and functional. Any noticeable flaws or repairs are transparently communicated.

Do you offer restoration services?

We believe in preserving the authenticity of our vintage furniture, so we generally do not offer extensive restoration services. However, we might perform minor touch-ups to enhance the aesthetics while maintaining the piece's original character.

How do I care for my purchased vintage furniture?

Vintage furniture requires special care. Keep the furniture away from direct sunlight, moisture, and extreme temperatures. Dust regularly and use appropriate cleaning products. Avoid harsh chemicals that can damage the original finish.

Can I view the furniture in person before purchasing?

Absolutely! We encourage customers to visit our showroom to see the furniture in person. This provides an opportunity to inspect the pieces closely, appreciate their craftsmanship, and make an informed decision.

Do you offer custom sourcing services?

At this time, we do not offer custom sourcing for specific vintage furniture pieces. However, we're always happy to hear about your preferences and might come across something that matches your criteria in the future.

How can I stay updated on new arrivals?

You can subscribe to our newsletter or follow us on social media platforms to receive notifications about our latest vintage furniture arrivals, promotions, and events.

Are the prices of your vintage furniture negotiable?

While we strive to offer fair and competitive prices for our vintage furniture, we understand that negotiations are sometimes possible. Feel free to reach out to us, and we'll consider your offer on a case-by-case basis.
