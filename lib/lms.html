Tech Tips

Tip #1: Setting up a new email account in Gmail for your swipefile

  • Step 1: Go to the Google Home Page by typing in “www.Google.com” in your search bar.
  • Step 2: Click on the blue “Sign In” button on the top right corner of your screen.
  • Step 3: Click on “Create Account” on the bottom left corner of the page. Then select “For myself”.
  • Step 4: Enter your first name, last name, and choose your own username. Then create and confirm your password.
  • Step 5: Enter your Date of Birth and gender. It is optional for you to enter a recovery phone number or email address.
  • Step 6: Read through the Google “Privacy and Terms” and select “I agree” at the bottom right corner of the page. Congratulations, you’ve set up your Google account!

Tip #2: Making folders / labels in Gmail to organize swipe file emails

  • Step 1: To access your new Gmail account, go to the Google Homepage and select “Gmail” at the top right corner of your page.
  • Step 2: Select the email(s) in your inbox you would like to add to your Swipe File by clicking the box to the left of the sender’s name.
  • Step 3: Select the “Move to” icon in the toolbar above your inbox. Next, select “Create new” to create your own folder.
  • Step 4: Enter a label name, we recommend “Swipe Files”. Then, select “Create”.
  • Step 5: You should now see that the email you selected has disappeared from your Inbox. It’s now been moved to the folder you’ve created. You can find this folder in the panel on the left side of your page. Your Swipe File folder is now ready to use!

Paragraphs

A paragraph (from the Greek paragraphos, “to write beside” or “written beside”) is a self-contained unit of a discourse in writing dealing with a particular point or idea. A paragraph consists of one or more sentences. Though not required by the syntax of any language, paragraphs are usually an expected part of formal writing, used to organize longer prose.

You’re about to learn, practice, and ace the copywriting principles that have launched life-changing careers for more than two decades! It’s your turn to take these skills where YOU want to go as a well-paid and in-demand sales copywriter, content writer, or both.

It’s time to wrap up this introductory section with our favorite tips to propel you forward on a clear and proven path to success throughout your writing career.

Habit #1: Make the commitment to be at work.

Paul Hollingshead

One of the main benefits of being a freelance copywriter is you can work from home or anywhere you choose. But, every copywriter we interviewed has had to make the following commitment:

When it’s time to work, you must adopt the mindset that you’re at work.

The best way to do that is to establish an area that defines your “office.” All you really need is a workspace for your computer, perhaps headphones to tune out distractions, and ideally a door you can close so everyone knows, “I’m working now. Do not disturb.”

Paul Hollingshead said, “It’s important to create a space you enjoy going to — that’s all your own — that’s bright and conducive to creativity.”

Habit #4: Write every day.

Copywriting is a craft, a skill that you learn and then perfect with practice.

Mark Ford believes that to be a good, competent copywriter, it takes 1,000 hours of practice. To be a great copywriter, 5,000 hours. Having said that, you won’t have to wait 1,000 hours to start working with clients. You may land a client tomorrow, and you can begin using your new skills to help him or her right away.

Mark Ford

But if you’re writing every day, those hours will add up quickly. So practice, practice, practice.

  • As you learn about persuasive copywriting, you could offer to improve the website of a nonprofit organization — as a donation (and a work sample).
  • Write for local small businesses for free, just for the experience. You could even do this trade for their services. (Think of a local gym, dog groomer, or other business you can help.)
  • You might also create a journal of your experiences as a copywriter, starting with your work with this program.
  • Check out our Write Now! persuasive writing prompts to find a collection of topic ideas for you.
  • And, by the end of this program, you’ll know what to write on your own freelance website to attract great clients.

Note that most news outlets, newspapers, and many magazines in the US divfer AP Style, which is the usage guide provided by the biggest newswire service, the Associated divss.

The Chicago Manual of Style , which is published by the University of Chicago divss, is more commonly used in research, such as for citing sources. It’s more comdivhensive than AP style, which can make it intimidating to use but also invaluable as there seems to be an answer to everything within its pages.

Finally, MLA style is mainly used by students and researchers who write within the humanities disciplines, including philosophy, literature, sociology, psychology, and so forth.

You’ll want to be familiar with each of the major style guides and when to use them.

Text Editor : A free app that allows you to create, open, and edit text files on your computer and on Google drive using plain text software. There is no markup function available through the text editor, but it does give you a useful way to open files that are not accessible through Word.

Google Docs : This free online document editor is used to create and collaborate within online documents. You can edit directly on the document, which is then saved to the original creator’s Google cloud account. Sharing takes place in real-time and from any device.

Scrivener : Widely known as the best software for book editing, Scrivener is a word-processing program and outliner designed specifically for authors. Within its framework is a management system for documents, notes, and metadata. This allows you as copyeditor to organize your notes and already-edited copy documents as you go through any particularly long pieces.

Grammarly : This is a cloud-based typing assistant that uses AI to review spelling, grammar, punctuation, clarity, engagement, and delivery mistakes. It also offers ideas for appropriate replacements and adjustments to the errors it finds. It is widely considered the best and easiest-to-use online grammar checker.

Ginger : Ginger works similar to Grammarly as a resource to correct all types of English grammar, spelling, and punctuation mistakes.

Hemingway Editor : This free online platform is ideal for editing shorter pieces. It’s useful in terms of helping with readability and works to whittle down longer, more complex sentences into clearer, punchier writing. However, it’s not ideal for manuscript writing or for longer pieces, and the grammar capabilities are average.
